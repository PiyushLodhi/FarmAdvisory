Indian mustard (Brassica juncea) is predominantly cultivated in Rajasthan, UP, Haryana, MP and Gujarat. 
Brown sarson is cultivated in Assam, Bihar, Orissa, and West Bengal. Gobhi sarson (B. napus L.) and karan rai (Brassica carinata) are the new emerging oilseed crops.
Mustard require an annual precipitation of 40-100 cm.
Mustard thrive well on light-loam soil.
For Mustard, Field should be ploughed by mould board plough or tractor drawn harrow. 
For Mustard, Before pre-sowing irrigation followed by two ploughing with cultivator are required to prepare good seed bed. 
There should be no clod or weeds at sowing time.
